01 — Game Identity

Core concept

A cooperative story-driven milsim FPS set in a fractured near-future Earth. Players are Watchdog operatives deployed by Vanguard Solutions into a city gone wrong. Over five story arcs they uncover a world-ending conspiracy called The Harvest — and must choose who they serve.

PvE campaign core. PvX endgame. 3-man fireteam throughout. Boots on the ground — no fantasy powers, no hard classes.

PvE Core PvX Endgame 3-man Fireteam Gear-based roles

Inspirations

Escape from Tarkov — story through environment, handler tension, side mission conscience, environmental lore
Space Marines 2 — upgrade mastery, squad feel, escalating threat, power progression
Destiny — raids, public events, seasonal live content, exotic loot chase
Dying Light — moral weight, defection mechanic, world as character, faction politics
Metal Gear Solid — PMC identity, corporate conspiracy, callsign culture, handler arc

Co-op faction rules

In story missions the host's faction applies to all squad members — a Covenant player joining a Severed story plays as Severed for that session. Personal faction only matters in your own HQ, open world, and solo play. Co-op access is never gated by faction alignment.

Mixed-faction squads are valid in all endgame content. The politics are for the open world.

Reputation system

Tracked per player, per faction. Earned through missions, side missions, and world events. Unlocks vendor access, faction dialogue, allied NPC appearances in HQ, and exclusive world event types.

Rep is personal — never shared with squadmates. Two players in the same fireteam can have opposing faction reputations.

Faction relationships

Covenant allies with Remnant Corps — military structure, shared resources, joint assault missions. Hostile to Severed, Changed, Architects, Wraith Division.
Severed allies with The Changed — mutual survival, underground network, bio-gear access. Hostile to Covenant, Remnant Corps, Architects, Wraith Division.
Wraith Division is hostile to everyone including Covenant operatives. They answer only to the corporation above Vanguard. They exist to erase loose ends — and both player factions are loose ends.
The Architects are hostile to all life without exception. A small number of defectors communicate through action and artefacts — they are the key to understanding the Harvest.
03 — Story & Campaign

The Harvest

The Architects — post-human former Vanguard subjects from Project Sable — are executing a directive called the Harvest: systematically archiving all complex matter on Earth before initiating the Fold, a collapse of this reality that opens a threshold to something on the other side.

The anomalies are collection sites where reality is being thinned for archiving. The Changed are humans accidentally exposed to Harvest energy near active sites — not a bio-weapon, but a preview of what the Harvest does to unprepared biology. Wraith Division knows, made a deal, and has been clearing people away from sites ever since. The handler knows, and stayed quiet.

Phase 1 — Survey

Active at game start. Anomalies appear. Architects observe and map. Collection sites seeded.

Phase 2 — Collection

Arc 3–4. Active archiving. Anomalies expand. The Changed multiply. Reality frays visibly.

Phase 3 — The Fold

Arc 5 threshold. Raids are set inside active Phase 3 collection sites.

Vanguard Solutions: Public face — private security contractor. Real face — Project Sable created the conditions for the Architects to exist. Wraith Division's deal with them: be in the archive. Survive the Fold as data. The corporation isn't purely evil — they made a terrible calculation and kept making it.

Side missions — the player's conscience

Five per arc. Never handler-assigned. Come from world contacts, unsecured channels, and environmental discovery. The handler doesn't know you did them. They reveal what Vanguard's orders are actually causing in the world and build rep with non-PMC factions.

Side missions are always optional. The world remembers — faction contacts appear in HQ, environments change, NPC dialogue shifts based on your history. Arc 1 sample side missions include: Shelter (civilian enclave cut off by Corps patrols), Deserters (Corps soldiers who abandoned their unit to survive), Frequency (civilian broadcasting about what they saw near the anomaly — first Harvest lore the handler never mentioned).

04 — Watchdog HQ

An abandoned logistics depot established in Mission 01. Persists and evolves through all arcs and post-story. The physical space reflects the campaign's progress — rooms unlock, evidence accumulates, faction contacts appear. It is the player's home for the entire game.

Handler station

Briefing screens, mission boards. Grows more surveillance-heavy through the arcs without comment from the handler.

Operations board

Main and side mission select. Side missions appear without handler flagging — sourced from the world, not Vanguard.

Lore archive

Physical items collected in the field. Datapads, comms logs, Architect artefacts. Buildable across the whole campaign.

Armoury

Loadout and weapon selection. Placeholder for the full gear system in a future update.

The back room

Locked at game start. Opens post-defection. The squad's real plans — away from the handler's screens.

Faction contacts

NPCs who appear as reputation grows. A Changed survivor. A Corps deserter. A defector Architect, eventually.

05 — Operative Identity

Character setup

Players input a real name at character creation. The game assigns a callsign from a curated pool — appropriate to the world's tone, safe for Roblox. The handler uses the real name in private briefings. Everyone else uses the callsign.

In a squad, individual callsigns are replaced by a shared squad callsign — you become a unit. A squad assigned SABLE without knowing what Project Sable was, and then finding out in Arc 3, is the kind of detail that rewards attention.

Dialogue system

Four tones. No morality meter — tone affects how the handler responds and what they inadvertently reveal, not whether the player succeeds. The handler's final Arc 3 speech changes based on which tone dominated the campaign.

Professional

Efficient, mission-focused. Handler's favourite early on — and the most unsettling to them by Arc 3 when you're still professional about things they hoped would rattle you.

Skeptical

Questions discrepancies. Handler over-explains, inadvertently revealing more than they intended. Unlocks additional handler dialogue throughout.

Defiant

Pushes back directly. Handler respects it early, grows nervous later. A consistently Defiant player gets flagged in a Wraith Division document found in Arc 3.

Observant

Describes what you notice. Unlocks unique environmental callouts and lore details mid-mission. Rewards players who pay attention to the world.

Launch customisation

Real name input. Game-assigned callsign. Roblox avatar base with tactical gear overlay. Faction cosmetic applied post-Arc 3 (Covenant or Severed visual identity, colour scheme, insignia). Default dialogue tone settable but overridable per conversation.

06 — Content Systems

Raid 1 — The Threshold Gate

Unlocks post-campaign. Set inside the Arc 1 anomaly, now fully expanded. Phase 3 is active inside — reality is visibly wrong. Three encounters. First look at what the Fold actually does to a place. 3-man. Weekly lockout, farmable after first clear.

Encounter 1 — The Collection Floor

Traversal + mechanics introduction

Navigate the outer collection site where Harvest energy is pulling matter apart and reassembling it. Mechanic: players carry anchor points that stabilise their position in reality while Architect Sentinels try to reclaim them. Drop the anchor and the world starts folding around you.

Anchor mechanicSentinel enemiesEnvironmental hazard

Encounter 2 — The Warden

Mid-boss — mechanic escalation

An Architect Warden initiates localised Fold events, temporarily pulling one player into a fragmented solo reality while the other two maintain anchors. Coordination required. The Warden has visible human proportions beneath its restructured exterior — a reminder of Project Sable.

Split mechanicSolo fragment phaseAnchor dependency

Encounter 3 — The Threshold

Final boss — all mechanics combined

The Architect Prime. The threshold is visible behind it — a tear in reality showing something on the other side the game never lets you see clearly. The defector Architect appears mid-fight to keep the threshold from fully opening. When the Prime dies, the defector closes the threshold. Looks at the squad. Leaves. No words. Raid ends.

World events

Harvest Ritual

Rare, unannounced. Architects perform a collection event in a live map. Players stumble into it. Interrupt for unique loot. Ignore it and nearby enemies are empowered.

Incursion (public event)

Timed, map-visible. All players in the world can join. Waves, objective, final boss. Hidden heroic trigger makes it harder with better rewards.

Seasonal events

Story-tied content drops. New map areas, faction activity, time-limited missions. Tied to raid narrative. New loot pool each season.

PvX — Covenant vs Severed

Unlocks post-Arc 3. All PvX is opt-in by entering contested zones. Both factions have access to all PvX activities.

Territory control

Key open world zones flip between factions on player action. Holding zones unlocks faction events and resources.

Bounty system

Generated automatically through PvX actions. See Section 09 for full bounty system design.

Extraction raids

Loyalists raid Severed safe houses. Asymmetric — attackers have numbers, defenders know the layout.

09 — Open World

World structure

Blacksite is an open world game with instanced exceptions. The city and its expanding districts are a shared persistent world — players encounter each other naturally, cooperate freely, and compete in PvX zones without loading screens. Only campaign missions (main and side), and raids are instanced. Everything else — world events, territory control, bounty hunts, faction activities, exploration — happens in the open world.

What is open world

All world events — Harvest Rituals, Incursions, seasonal activities

Territory control and PvX zones

Bounty system — hunting and being hunted

Faction rep activities and allied NPC encounters

Exploration, environmental lore, anomaly sites

HQ — shared with invited players

What is instanced

All campaign missions — main and side, Arc 1–5

All raids — 3-man private instances

Everything else is shared and persistent

Districts — world expands with story

The open world grows as arcs unlock. Each arc opens a new district — players who've completed further arcs can explore ahead, but story content within those districts is gated to players who've reached them. The world always feels larger than the current mission.

10 — Mission 1 & Watchdog HQ

Mission 1 — Beachhead

The game opens directly into a cutscene. No menu. No loading screen preamble. The player spawns into the world mid-briefing — the handler's voice comes in over comms before the screen fully fades up. The tutorial and story are the same thing from the first second.

Cutscene opening

A transport. Darkness. The handler's voice — calm, professional, reading coordinates. The operative's hands check a weapon in the dark. The ramp drops. A fractured city skyline. First dialogue choice appears before boots hit the ground — the callsign moment. The handler assigns it based on your response. You never choose it yourself.

No tutorial pop-ups during the cutscene. The game trusts the player to move when it's time to move.

Mission structure

Objective 1 — Clear the Remnant Corps forward camp on the city outskirts. Combat tutorial embedded naturally in the encounter design.

Objective 2 — Push to the logistics depot. Handler guides via comms. Environmental lore begins — something happened here before the Corps arrived.

Objective 3 — Secure and establish the depot as Watchdog HQ. The mission ends when the HQ is operational. The world opens.

What unlocks after Mission 1

HQ fully accessible — all ground floor rooms open

Mission 2 available on the operations board

Open world Industrial District accessible

HQ invite function unlocked

Side missions begin appearing on the board

First lore item placed in the archive

Watchdog HQ — detail

Established in Mission 1. The physical space evolves through all five arcs and persists post-story. Rooms unlock, evidence accumulates on the walls, faction contacts appear. It is the player's home for the entire game — and the one place the open world can't touch.

Invite system

The squad leader invites players by Roblox username. Invited players join the HQ session as guests — they can access the operations board, lore archive, and armoury, but the HQ's faction alignment and story state reflect the owner's progress. A player further behind in the story visiting a more advanced HQ can see rooms and contacts they haven't unlocked yet — a deliberate spoiler-lite preview that rewards exploration.

HQ sessions are private by default. The leader can open slots at any time. Maximum occupancy mirrors the fireteam cap — 3 players.

Rooms & evolution

Handler station — Active from Mission 1. Briefing screens, mission board. Surveillance equipment appears quietly through Arc 2–3 without comment.
Operations board — Main and side mission select. Side missions appear from non-handler sources — no Vanguard flagging.
Lore archive — Physical artefacts, datapads, comms logs. Builds across the full campaign. The Arc 1 Mission 10 object lives here.
Armoury — Loadout selection. Placeholder for full gear system update.
Faction contacts — NPCs who appear as rep grows. Changed survivor, Corps deserter, defector Architect eventually.
The back room — Locked until post-defection. The squad's real operations, away from handler screens.
11 — Bounty System

How it works

Inspired by The Division's Dark Zone and Sea of Thieves. The bounty system creates emergent PvP tension in the open world without forcing it on PvE players. It operates on two sides — the Marked (players with active bounties) and Bounty Hunters (players who opt in to hunt them).

Getting a bounty — the Marked

Bounties are generated automatically through PvX actions in the open world. No opt-in required — if you're playing aggressively in the world, you attract attention.

Capturing territory from the opposing faction

Killing opposing faction players in contested zones

Disrupting a Harvest Ritual or Incursion event

High faction rep actions that draw Wraith Division attention

Bounty value scales with the actions that generated it. A player who captured three territories and killed five opponents has a significantly higher bounty than someone who contested a single zone.

Becoming a Bounty Hunter

Bounty Hunters opt in by interacting with a Bounty Board — physical boards placed in the open world, one per district. Toggle on, toggle off. Once active:

You always know where your target is — a directional indicator and approximate distance. No hiding.

You are flagged as a Bounty Hunter to other players — visible status

Killing your target turns in the bounty automatically — reward issued on kill

You can toggle off at any Bounty Board — exits the hunter role

Non-hunters are never targeted by the bounty system. PvE players can ignore it entirely.

Bounty value — grow and shrink

A bounty is not binary. It has a value that grows and shrinks based on ongoing behaviour.

Grows — continued PvX actions, territory grabs, kills, surviving as a target

Grows — completing bounty events while marked (see below)

Shrinks slightly — being killed by a Bounty Hunter. Not reset, just reduced. You keep your history.

Clears — turning yourself in at a faction contact for a partial payout, or the bounty naturally decaying if you go inactive

The longer you survive as a Marked player the more valuable your bounty becomes — creating a risk/reward loop where the highest-value targets are also the most experienced at not getting caught.

Bounty events — in-world activities

Special open world events exist specifically for the bounty ecosystem. Both Marked players and Bounty Hunters can participate.

Contraband drops — A supply cache appears in a contested zone. Marked players who secure it increase their bounty value. Hunters who intercept it get a bonus payout.

Signal flares — A Marked player who activates a flare broadcasts their location to all Hunters — in exchange for a large bounty value increase if they survive the resulting attention window.

Extraction point — A timed window where a Marked player can cash out their full bounty value by reaching an extraction point. Hunters know where it is. High risk, best reward.

Dying in a bounty event does not reset the bounty. It only costs the event reward — the bounty value itself carries forward.

Rewards

Marked players earn

Escalating bounty-exclusive cosmetic rewards for reaching value thresholds. The longer you survive the more you unlock. Turning in or cashing out pays faction currency. Unique titles — "Ghost", "The Hunted", "Marked" — earned by surviving at high bounty value.

Bounty Hunters earn

Payout scales with the bounty value of the target killed. Hunter-exclusive cosmetics for total value collected across all hunts. A ranked Hunter leaderboard per season — top Hunters get unique seasonal rewards.

What bounties never affect

Gear, stats, story progress, faction reputation, raid access. The bounty system is entirely cosmetic and currency reward. PvE players who never engage with it lose nothing. It is additive, never punitive.

12 — Future Systems (post-launch)

Gear system

Six rarity tiers with levellable perks earned through use. Drop-based. Mod slots on Epic and above. Exotic limit: one weapon, one armour piece simultaneously — hard cap, never broken.

Common — base stats, no perks

Uncommon — 1 perk slot

Rare — 2 perk slots

Epic — 3 perks + 1 mod slot

Legendary — 3 perks + 2 mod slots + unique trait

Exotic — unique exotic perk, 1 per loadout slot max

Skill system

Tarkov-style use-based progression. No classes — gear defines role. Skills level through doing. Cap per level bracket so you can't max everything early.

Endurance, Recoil Control, Medical Speed, Stealth, Heavy Weapons and more

A player who keeps patching teammates becomes a better medic. A player who always flanks becomes harder to detect. Playstyle shapes the soldier.

PvP expansion

Built on the PvX foundation. Structured modes — extraction, territory control, bounty ranked. Potential for Wraith Division as a PvP antagonist NPC faction targeting both sides in contested zones.

Deep customisation (post-launch)

Cosmetic gear

Weapon skins, armour sets, helmet variants. Faction-aesthetic sets — Remnant Corps surplus, Changed bio-armour aesthetic, Architect-derived energy gear.

Title system

"Ghost of Sable", "The Severed", "Watchdog Actual" — earned through gameplay, displayed under callsign. Tells other players your history at a glance.

Emblems & patches

Earned by completing story arcs, raids, and side mission chains. Displayed on armour. Callsign patch visual on gear. Cosmetic only — never gameplay.
